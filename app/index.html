The outcome surprised many observers, as polls had consistently shown Vice President Harris leading in the months before election day, with her message of generational change resonating strongly with voters.

But should we really be surprised?

The dramatic shift in Democratic leadership mid-campaign created an unprecedented scenario that traditional polls struggled to capture accurately.

We're going to examine prediction markets alongside traditional polling data to see how they reacted to key events throughout the campaign.

This analysis will help us understand whether prediction markets may be better indicators of election outcomes compared to traditional polls, especially in scenarios of significant political upheaval.

What are Prediction Markets?

Prediction markets are like betting markets for real-world events. Instead of betting on sports, people bet on election outcomes.

How do they work?

Buying

If you think Trump will win and his contracts cost $0.60, you can buy them. If he wins, each contract pays $1.00.

Selling

If you think Trump will lose, you can sell contracts. If he loses, contracts are worth $0.00 and you keep the money.

The price of a contract shows what people think will happen. A price of $0.60 means the market thinks there's a 60% chance of winning.

Why might prediction markets be better than polls? Because people are betting real money, they:

  • Think carefully before betting
  • React quickly to new information
  • Consider all available evidence

Arbitrage Opportunities

Arbitrage is a simple but powerful concept: it's the ability to profit from price differences between markets.

Imagine you could buy a Trump contract for $0.50 on one market and simultaneously sell it for $0.52 on another. That $0.02 difference is pure profit, with no risk!

Below, we track these opportunities between Kalshi and Polymarket throughout the campaign. The slider lets you explore different moments in time:

  • Total Cost: How much you need to spend to execute the arbitrage
  • Potential Profit: The guaranteed profit per $1 invested
  • Max Volume: The maximum number of contracts available
  • Max Profit: The total profit possible at this moment
